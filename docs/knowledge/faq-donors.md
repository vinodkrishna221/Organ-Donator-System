# FAQ for Organ Donors

## General Questions

### Q: Who can donate organs?
**A:** Almost anyone can be a potential donor regardless of age or medical history. Medical suitability is determined at the time of death by medical professionals. Living donors must be:
- 18 years or older
- In good health
- Capable of giving informed consent

### Q: What is the difference between living and deceased donation?
**A:** 
- **Living Donation**: You donate while alive. In India, living donors can only donate one kidney.
- **Deceased Donation**: Organs are donated after brain death or cardiac death. Multiple organs can be donated.

### Q: Can I specify which organs I want to donate?
**A:** Yes, you can specify which organs you wish to donate. However, organ availability depends on medical suitability at the time of donation.

### Q: Is there any cost to the donor's family?
**A:** No. All costs related to organ recovery are borne by the recipient or the hospital. The donor's family never pays for organ donation.

### Q: Will organ donation affect my funeral arrangements?
**A:** No. Organ donation does not affect funeral arrangements. The body is treated with respect and care.

## Living Donation

### Q: Can I donate a kidney to a stranger?
**A:** Living donation to strangers (altruistic donation) requires approval from the Authorization Committee. You must prove there is no commercial transaction involved.

### Q: What is the recovery time for kidney donors?
**A:** Most living kidney donors can resume normal activities within 2-4 weeks, though complete recovery may take 4-6 weeks.

### Q: Are there any health risks for living donors?
**A:** Living kidney donation is generally safe. Donors can live healthy lives with one kidney. However, there are surgical risks and long-term monitoring is recommended.

## Deceased Donation

### Q: What is brain death?
**A:** Brain death is the irreversible cessation of all brain function, including the brain stem. It is legally recognized as death in India. A team of 4 doctors must certify brain death.

### Q: How many organs can be donated after death?
**A:** Up to 8 organs can be transplanted from a single deceased donor:
- 2 kidneys
- 1 liver (can be split for 2 recipients)
- 1 heart
- 2 lungs
- 1 pancreas
- 2 corneas

### Q: How do I register as an organ donor?
**A:** You can register through:
1. NOTTO website (https://notto.gov.in)
2. State OTTO websites
3. Participating hospitals
4. NGOs working in organ donation

Remember: Inform your family about your decision to donate.
