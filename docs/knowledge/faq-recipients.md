# FAQ for Organ Recipients

## General Questions

### Q: How do I get on the transplant waiting list?
**A:** To be listed for an organ transplant:
1. Get referred by your doctor to a transplant center
2. Undergo medical evaluation
3. Receive approval from the transplant team
4. Get registered with your state OTTO/SOTTO

### Q: How long will I wait for an organ?
**A:** Wait times vary based on:
- Organ needed
- Blood type
- Medical urgency
- Geographic location
- Availability of compatible donors

Kidney wait times in India can range from a few months to several years.

### Q: How are organs allocated?
**A:** Organs are allocated based on:
1. Medical urgency
2. Blood type compatibility
3. Time on waiting list
4. Geographic proximity
5. Body size match

### Q: Can I choose my donor?
**A:** For deceased donation, you cannot choose the donor - organs are allocated through the official system. For living donation, you may receive from:
- Near relatives without committee approval
- Non-relatives with Authorization Committee approval

## Medical Questions

### Q: What medical tests are required?
**A:** Recipients typically undergo:
- Blood tests (blood type, tissue typing)
- Heart evaluation (ECG, echocardiogram)
- Lung tests
- Liver and kidney function tests
- Psychological evaluation
- Cancer screening

### Q: What is tissue matching?
**A:** Tissue matching (HLA matching) compares proteins on cells. Better matches reduce rejection risk. Perfect matches are rare, but modern immunosuppressive drugs make transplants successful even with imperfect matches.

### Q: What happens after a transplant?
**A:** After transplant:
1. Hospital stay (varies by organ)
2. Lifelong immunosuppressive medications
3. Regular follow-up appointments
4. Healthy lifestyle maintenance
5. Monitoring for rejection signs

## Financial Questions

### Q: What are the costs involved?
**A:** Costs include:
- Pre-transplant evaluation
- Transplant surgery
- Hospital stay
- Medications (lifelong)
- Follow-up care

Many government schemes and insurance plans cover transplant costs.

### Q: Is there financial assistance available?
**A:** Yes:
- Ayushman Bharat covers kidney and liver transplants
- State government health schemes
- Hospital charity programs
- NGO support

### Q: Who pays for the donor's expenses?
**A:** For deceased donation, the recipient or their insurance covers organ recovery costs. For living donation, typically the recipient covers donor evaluation and surgery costs.
