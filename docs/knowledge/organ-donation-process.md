# Organ Donation Process in India

## Overview

The organ donation process in India follows specific protocols to ensure ethical, transparent, and medically sound procedures.

---

## Living Donation Process

### Step 1: Donor Evaluation
1. Initial health screening
2. Blood type and tissue matching tests
3. Comprehensive medical examination
4. Psychological evaluation
5. Financial assessment (to ensure no coercion)

### Step 2: Relationship Verification
- **Near Relatives** (spouse, parents, children, siblings, grandparents):
  - Proof of relationship (marriage certificate, birth certificate)
  - Direct approval from transplant hospital
  
- **Non-Near Relatives**:
  - Application to Authorization Committee
  - Prove "affection or attachment" with donor
  - Committee interview and approval

### Step 3: Pre-Surgery Preparation
1. Final medical clearances
2. Consent form signing
3. Surgery scheduling
4. Pre-operative instructions

### Step 4: Surgery and Recovery
1. Donor surgery (minimally invasive when possible)
2. Hospital stay (3-5 days for kidney)
3. Follow-up appointments
4. Complete recovery (4-6 weeks)

---

## Deceased Donation Process

### Step 1: Brain Death Identification
1. Patient identified as potential candidate
2. Initial assessment by treating physician
3. Notification to transplant coordinator

### Step 2: Brain Death Certification
A panel of 4 doctors must certify brain death:
1. Medical Superintendent/Head of Hospital
2. Treating physician (not from transplant team)
3. Neurologist/Neurosurgeon
4. Independent doctor nominated by government

Two tests conducted 6 hours apart.

### Step 3: Family Consent
1. Transplant coordinator approaches family
2. Explanation of donation process
3. Written consent obtained
4. Respect family's decision either way

### Step 4: Donor Assessment
1. Medical history review
2. Lab tests (blood type, infections, organ function)
3. Organ-specific imaging
4. Determine viable organs

### Step 5: Organ Allocation
1. Enter into NOTTO/ROTTO system
2. Match with waiting recipients
3. Prioritize based on urgency, compatibility, waiting time
4. Notify recipient hospitals

### Step 6: Organ Retrieval
1. Surgical team retrieves organs
2. Organs preserved in special solutions
3. Transport to recipient hospitals
4. Time-sensitive (varies by organ):
   - Heart: 4-6 hours
   - Liver: 12-15 hours
   - Kidneys: 24-48 hours

### Step 7: Transplantation
1. Recipient prepared for surgery
2. Transplant surgery performed
3. Post-operative monitoring
4. Begin immunosuppression

## Organs That Can Be Donated

| Organ | Living Donation | Deceased Donation | Viability Time |
|-------|-----------------|-------------------|----------------|
| Kidney | ✓ (1 only) | ✓ (both) | 24-48 hours |
| Liver | Partial (rare) | ✓ | 12-15 hours |
| Heart | ✗ | ✓ | 4-6 hours |
| Lungs | ✗ | ✓ | 4-6 hours |
| Pancreas | ✗ | ✓ | 12-24 hours |
| Cornea | ✗ | ✓ | 14 days |

---

## Key Contact Points

- **NOTTO**: National coordination
- **ROTTO**: Regional coordination  
- **SOTTO**: State coordination
- **Hospital Transplant Coordinator**: Local facilitation

---

## Important Notes

1. Organ donation is always free - it's illegal to buy or sell organs
2. Family's wishes are respected if they refuse donation
3. All religions support organ donation
4. There is no age limit for donation - suitability is medical
5. One deceased donor can save up to 8 lives
