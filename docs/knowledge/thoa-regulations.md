# Transplantation of Human Organs Act (THOA)

## Overview

The Transplantation of Human Organs Act, 1994 (amended in 2011) is the primary legislation governing organ donation and transplantation in India.

## Key Provisions

### Legal Framework

1. **Brain Death Recognition**: Legal recognition of brain death as a form of death
2. **Regulation of Removal**: Regulates removal of human organs for therapeutic purposes
3. **Storage and Transplantation**: Governs storage and transplantation procedures
4. **Authorization Committees**: Mandatory approval for living donor transplants

### Types of Donation

#### 1. Living Donation
- **Near Relatives**: Spouse, parents, children, siblings (no committee approval needed)
- **Other Than Near Relatives**: Requires authorization committee approval
- **Allowed Organs**: Only one kidney from living donors
- **Age Requirement**: Must be 18 years or older

#### 2. Deceased Donation
- **Brain Death**: Donor declared brain dead by authorized medical board
- **Cardiac Death**: After circulatory death (limited organs)
- **Allowed Organs**: Multiple organs (kidney, liver, heart, lungs, pancreas, cornea, etc.)

### Authorization Committee

Required for living donations from non-near relatives:
1. Must prove relationship or close association
2. Evaluate donor's motivation
3. Ensure no commercial transaction
4. Committee consists of medical professionals and government officials

## Penalties

- **Organ Trafficking**: 3-5 years imprisonment + fine up to ₹20 lakhs
- **Unauthorized Removal**: 5-10 years imprisonment + fine up to ₹1 crore

## Recent Amendments (2011)

1. Swap donation legalized
2. Retrieval centers can be separate from transplant centers
3. Expanded definition of "near relative" to include grandparents
4. Mandatory reporting of brain deaths
